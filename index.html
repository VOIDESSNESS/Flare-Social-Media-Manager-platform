Knowledge Base

UK Fire Law Hub

A social media manager for fire safety must know the law as well as any compliance officer. This hub gives you the working knowledge to post with authority, accuracy, and legal precision. Each law has a ready-made content hook.

RRO 2005 — England & Wales
Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005
The master legislation. Every non-domestic premise and multi-occupied residential building must have a designated Responsible Person who conducts, documents, and reviews fire risk assessments. Failure = criminal prosecution, unlimited fines.
"Post hook: 'Do you know who the Responsible Person is in your building? Under UK law, it's not optional — it's your legal duty.'"
2022 — England
Fire Safety (England) Regulations 2022
Direct response to Grenfell. Introduced quarterly fire door checks, lift inspections, and resident engagement duties for all multi-occupied residential buildings. High-rise buildings (18m+) have additional monthly obligations.
"Post hook: 'Since January 2023, quarterly fire door checks are legally required in blocks of flats. Is your building compliant?'"
2022 — England
Building Safety Act 2022
Landmark reform creating the Building Safety Regulator. High-rise buildings (18m+ / 7+ storeys) now have an Accountable Person with duties to register, assess, and manage building safety. The Golden Thread of building information is now a legal requirement.
"Post hook: 'The Building Safety Act 2022 is the biggest overhaul of building law in 40 years. Here's what it means for your property.'"
SI 2022/547 — England
Smoke & CO Alarm (Amendment) Regulations 2022
Mandatory CO alarms in every room with a fixed combustion appliance (excluding gas cookers) in all rented properties. Alarms must be tested at the start of every tenancy. Civil penalty up to £5,000 for non-compliance.
"Post hook: 'If you rent out a property with a gas boiler and no CO alarm — you are breaking the law. Here's what changed in October 2022.'"
2015 — England
Smoke & CO Alarm (England) Regulations 2015
Required smoke alarms on every storey of rented properties and CO alarms in rooms with solid fuel appliances. The 2022 amendment extended this significantly. Every new tenancy requires a test at the start.
"Post hook: 'Your landlord is legally required to fit smoke alarms on every floor of your rented home. Do yours work?'"
Housing Act 2004 — UK
HMO Licensing & Management Regulations
Mandatory licensing for HMOs with 5+ occupants. Fire safety provisions include FD30S fire doors, automatic fire detection (BS 5839-6), and emergency lighting. Local authority may impose additional conditions. Unlimited fine for operating without a licence.
"Post hook: 'Running an HMO without the correct fire doors? Your licence — and your freedom — could be at risk. Here's the full legal picture.'"
